In order to provide access to its collections, the George Eastman Museum makes available many of these objects to recognized institutions and exhibition agencies, such as libraries and museums, which have established exhibition programs, an acceptable physical environment, and a full–time professional staff proficient in handling the particular material requested. Each request for loan will be judged on its particular merits—the type of institution, the program, and the exhibition for which the loan is proposed in relation to the importance, stability, uniqueness, and value (both monetary and intrinsic) of the material being requested.

For more information on loans, select the specific type of loan you would like to make to start the process.

Moving Image +

Contact the loan coordinator at loans@eastman.org with a list of desired film titles and screening dates. The museum will notify you of the availability of the prints or media, and provide details for next steps

Photography +

In order to process your loan, we will need:

1. A formal request letter addressed to Dr. Bruce Barnes, the director of the George Eastman Museum, with the name of the exhibition, its venues and display dates, a description of the exhibition and justification for why these particular objects have been selected for inclusion, whether a catalogue will be published, and confirmation that your institution, as the borrower, will cover the costs of the loan (including transportation and insurance.

2. A listing of the objects requested, including photographer (or maker), title, date, and accession number of the work(s) you would like to borrow. Please obtain the accession numbers from Ross Knapper, collection manager. You may also use our collections online. Note that all loaned photographs are shipped (and must remain) in sealed frames. Frames must remain sealed throughout the loan period.

3. The AAM General Facility Report(s) for your exhibition venue(s) and storage facilities. These should include temperature and humidity readings from the seasonal period corresponding to the loan period (e.g. if the exhibition takes place April – June 2017, the readings should be for April – June 2016).

Our loan policy stipulates that we receive loan requests eight months in advance of the exhibition's opening date, but exceptions are sometimes made.
